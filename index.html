Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the Solar System. It has a rocky surface covered with craters, similar to the Moon. Because it lacks a significant atmosphere, temperatures swing drastically—from extremely hot during the day to freezing at night. A single day on Mercury (sunrise to sunrise) lasts about 176 Earth days.It shows you are not small—you are just space-sized!.

Venus

Venus is often called Earth’s “sister planet” because of its similar size and structure. However, its thick carbon dioxide atmosphere traps heat through a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet. Its surface is covered with volcanoes and lava plains. Venus also rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction (retrograde rotation)..

Earth

Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has a balanced atmosphere rich in nitrogen and oxygen, and liquid water covers most of its surface. Earth’s magnetic field protects it from harmful solar radiation. It also has one natural satellite, the Moon, which influences tides and stabilizes the planet’s tilt.

Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface. It has a thin atmosphere mostly made of carbon dioxide and experiences dust storms that can cover the entire planet. Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the tallest volcano, and Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyon systems. Evidence suggests water once flowed on Mars.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System and is made mostly of hydrogen and helium. It has a powerful magnetic field and at least 90 known moons, including Ganymede—the largest moon in the Solar System. The Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has been raging for over 300 years.

Saturn

Saturn is best known for its stunning ring system, composed of ice, rock, and dust particles. It is a gas giant with a low density—so low that it could float in water (theoretically). Saturn has over 80 moons, with Titan being the largest, known for its thick atmosphere and methane lakes.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant with a unique tilt of about 98 degrees, causing it to rotate on its side. This leads to extreme seasonal variations. Its pale blue color comes from methane gas in its atmosphere. Uranus has faint rings and a system of moons orbiting around its tilted axis.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and an ice giant similar to Uranus. It has a deep blue color and is known for having the fastest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds over 2,000 km/h. Neptune has several moons, the largest being Triton, which orbits in the opposite direction of the planet’s rotation.
